1 point Feature
Survivor Accounts: Does the training include first-hand accounts or case studies shared by survivors?
Tailored Content: Is the content customized for specific roles (e.g., housekeeping vs. front desk vs. security)?
Statistics: Does the training provide verified data, facts, and current statistics on trafficking?

2 point Feature
Victim-Focused: Is the primary focus of the training on the well-being and safety of the victim rather than the perpetrator?
Risk Reduction: Does the training explicitly instruct employees not to intervene personally but to contact authorities instead?
Partnerships: Does the training mention or involve partnerships with vetted anti-trafficking NGOs or shelters?

3 point Feature
Overall Definition: Does the training provide clear definitions, real crime scene photos, and comprehensive examples of what trafficking is?
Trafficking Signs: Does it list specific indicators (e.g., constant supervision of guests, excessive trash, denial of cleaning, multiple guests in one room)?
Law Enforcement: Is there a clear protocol for reporting to law enforcement, including what details to record (room numbers, descriptions, timestamps)?
Legal Regulations: Does it cover the legalities of the crime, the risks of "wrongful suspicion," and how to handle a potentially complicit employee?
